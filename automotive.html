Automotive vs Counterfeits

Leading manufacturers of high-performance automotive components are frequently targeted by counterfeiters, resulting in the spread of imitation parts ranging from fake discs and pads to unauthorized branded accessories that were never part of the original product lineup.

Why Counterfeits Are Dangerous

Most of the counterfeit automotive components — such as braking parts — lack inadequate design and quality control, and thus contribute to significant safety risks, including reduced performance, increased wear, and potential structural failure.

Multilevel Counterfeit Threats

Counterfeit automotive parts can be classified as:

  • Fakes – Crude replicas of pads, discs, and calipers, lacking performance or safety.
  • Semi‑fakes – Genuine components salvaged from other vehicles, repainted and mis-branded, without verification for proper system compatibility.
  • Super‑fakes – Items never produced by the original manufacturer, such as decorative accessories, falsely bearing the brand's logo.

Anti‑Counterfeit Strategy implemented by Brands

To counter the rise of counterfeit products, industry leaders have introduced several authenticity verification measures for consumers:

  • Scratch‑off cards with unique codes included in the packaging, allowing buyers to verify products online.
  • Holograms & QR codes on components accompanied by tamper-evident seals and compliance markings.
  • Mobile apps that enable users to scan QR codes, confirming product authenticity and detecting potential counterfeits.
